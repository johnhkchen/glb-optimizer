## Context

The `assets/rose_julia_child.glb` model (7.8M) is a TRELLIS-generated Julia Child rose bush — dense, organic foliage that cannot be meaningfully represented by simple primitives. Decimation alone produces ugly results at the ratios needed for mobile scenes.

The approach is volumetric distillation: treat the dense model like an MRI subject, slicing it into layered cross-sections and rendering each slice as an alpha-textured quad. Stacking 4-8 textured planes reconstructs the visual impression of the plant at a fraction of the geometric cost. This is conceptually similar to billboard clouds / impostors but optimized offline for maximum quality within a strict triangle budget.

The priority is CPU/GPU friendliness at render time — minimal polygon count, alpha-textured quads, no complex shaders. The heavy work happens at bake time, not at runtime.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Analyze `rose_julia_child.glb` to determine optimal slicing strategy (vertical, radial, or hybrid based on plant structure)
- Generate a set of 6-8 textured quads (slices) that reconstruct the rose bush's visual appearance
- Each slice captures the color and alpha from the original model at that depth
- Output GLB is <100KB with embedded textures (vs 7.8M original)
- Triangle count: 12-16 triangles (2 per quad) for the base representation
- Visual quality: recognizable as a rose bush from typical garden-scene viewing angles and distances
- Demonstrate the approach works in the optimizer's Three.js preview (alpha blending, correct depth sorting)
- Document the slicing parameters and how they affect quality vs cost tradeoff
