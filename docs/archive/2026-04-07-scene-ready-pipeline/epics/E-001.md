## From Inedible Muffins to Photo-Real

### Thesis

The bake pipeline works. We've made *inedible muffins at the right price* — assets that hit the triangle/memory budget but don't yet have the visual flavor a landscape designer would put in front of a client. The next epic doesn't search for new techniques. It (a) makes the existing pipeline tunable by humans, (b) captures every tuning interaction as future training data, and (c) makes the pipeline geometry-aware so it adapts to whatever shape comes in.

### Scale Vision (north star, not immediate scope)

The pipeline we're building should not foreclose on future use cases at radically larger scale. Plausible directions, none of which are in scope for this epic but all of which inform architectural decisions:

- **Plant phenotyping**: 100,000 photos of *Oryza sativa* with site-location annotations → 10T synthetic plant models with <2% reference variance, annotations preserved
- **Landscape architecture**: every plant species on record in Oregon, baked once, reusable across thousands of design projects
- **Nature conservatories**: digitized herbaria with volumetric MRI data, baked to budget-friendly assets
- **Crop modeling**: time-series of growth stages per species, generated from dated reference photos

The data corpora we can ingest (plant name + text + photos + MRI volumetric data) are massive. The bottleneck is not data — it's tuning quality and the human effort per asset. This epic addresses both by making humans faster and capturing what they do.

### Two Architectural Decisions

**1. Analytics is foundational, not a track.** Every tuning slider movement, every regenerate, every accept/discard becomes a structured event. Stored locally as JSONL with linked thumbnails. Format spec'd up front to be both immediately usable (dashboards) AND ML-ready (features + actions + outcomes). Analytics has to land before most tuning UI ships, otherwise we lose the data from the first cohort of users.

**2. Geometry-aware via PCA-style analysis.** The high-poly input model is the source of truth for *what shape this is*. PCA on the vertex cloud → principal axes → shape classification (round-bush, directional, tall-narrow, planar, hard-surface) → routes to the right slicing/orientation strategy. No user labeling required. When classification is ambiguous, render multiple strategies side-by-side and let the user pick — every comparison becomes a labeled training example.

### Wave Order

- **Wave 0**: S-002 (foundational, blocks everything)
- **Wave 1**: S-003 (depends on S-002, instruments it)
- **Wave 2**: S-004, S-005, S-007 (parallel, all depend on S-002 + S-003)
- **Wave 3**: S-006 (depends on S-004 for shape categories)
- **Wave 4**: S-008 (consolidates everything that came before)

### Cross-cutting Requirements

- Every new feature ships with analytics event payloads — no "we'll instrument it later"
- Every tuning control has a sensible default that reproduces current behavior
- Profiles are first-class: named, reasoned-about, comment-able, taggable
- When a user accepts an asset, its current settings get marked as the canonical config — that tag is the most valuable training signal we capture

### Out of Scope (deferred to follow-up epics)

- Branching/topology classification (Y-shaped plants) beyond the basic taxonomy
- Centralized analytics upload — local-only for now
- Multi-asset scene-level tuning workflows
- Time-series / growth-stage workflows
- Hardscape rendering presets beyond the directional category
